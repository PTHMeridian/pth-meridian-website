PTH Meridian — AMuN Decision Science

Decisions have consequences.

Most decisions look simple until the consequences arrive. AMuN — Applied Mindshift Universal Navigator — makes the invisible visible. Map the second and third order consequences of any decision before you make it. See what you cannot see until it is too late to change.

Build data centers in water-stressed Alberta

// AMuN — the tool
Make the invisible visible.

Every decision has consequences that ripple outward — first order effects that are obvious, second order effects that are predictable if you look, third order effects that surprise everyone. Most decisions are made looking only at the first order. AMuN maps all three before the decision is final.

The tool is built on a simple principle — you cannot make a good decision without seeing what the decision actually does. Not what you intend it to do. What it actually does, to what systems, over what time horizon, under what conditions.

AMuN is open source and lives at github.com/PTHMeridian/AMuN. The interface asks one question first: what decision are you trying to make? Everything else follows from that.

Where AMuN applies

Every decision. Every scale.

Consequence mapping applies wherever decisions have consequences that are not immediately visible. Which is everywhere.

// Policy
Government Decisions

Infrastructure investment, energy policy, healthcare reform, trade agreements — government decisions affect millions of people across decades. AMuN maps the consequences before commitments are made and billions are spent.

// Business
Strategic Planning

Market entry, supply chain decisions, technology investment, partnership agreements — business decisions that look straightforward often have supply chain, regulatory and reputational consequences that arrive years later.

// Energy
Infrastructure Siting

Where to build a data center. Whether to approve a pipeline. How to design a national energy transition. These decisions have water, climate, economic and community consequences that standard analysis misses.

// Health
Health Policy

Drug approval, treatment protocol changes, public health interventions, healthcare system reforms — health decisions have economic, behavioural and equity consequences that compound over years and affect populations unevenly.

// Individual
Personal Decisions

Career changes, financial decisions, relationship choices, educational paths — AMuN's consequence mapping applies at every scale. The question is always the same: what does this decision actually do, beyond what I intend?

// Reconciliation
Indigenous Relations

Resource development on Indigenous territories, consultation processes, treaty implementation, energy infrastructure approvals — decisions with profound historical and community consequences that require full consequence visibility before proceeding.

// The insight behind AMuN
Education is not intelligence.

The people who designed Canadian residential school policy were educated men of their time. Credentialed. Expert. Certain of their conclusions. They were catastrophically wrong — not because they lacked information but because they never examined their foundational assumptions. They optimized for a goal that was itself wrong.

AMuN does not tell you what goal to have. It does not make the decision for you. It shows you what the decision actually does — across all the systems it touches, over all the time horizons that matter — so that the assumptions are visible rather than hidden.

The most dangerous decisions in history were not made in ignorance. They were made with great certainty, by educated people, who were optimizing for a goal that should have been examined before the optimization began.

Never confuse education with intelligence. You can have a PhD and still be an idiot.
— Richard Feynman
// Design principles
01
No Hidden Complexity
The tool shows everything it knows about a decision's consequences. No black boxes. No unexplained scores. Every consequence is traceable to its source.
02
Accessible To Everyone
The first question AMuN asks is simple enough for anyone to answer. The depth reveals itself. You do not need a PhD to use a consequence map. Good decisions should not require specialist credentials.
03
Neutral On Outcomes
AMuN does not tell you what to decide. It maps what will happen if you do. The judgment is yours. The information is complete.
04
Open Source
Every assumption in the model is visible and challengeable. The tool that is supposed to surface hidden assumptions cannot itself be a black box.
05
Causation Not Correlation
AMuN traces causal chains — not statistical associations. The difference between knowing that two things happen together and knowing why one causes the other is the difference between understanding and pattern-matching.
